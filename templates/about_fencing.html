About Fencing

What Is Fencing

Fencing is a sport with a rich history, blending strategy, precision, and athleticism. Originating from the ancient practice of swordsmanship, it has evolved into a modern Olympic sport.

There are three forms of modern fencing, each using a different kind of weapon and having different rules:

  • Foil: A lightweight weapon targeting the torso, with points scored by the tip.
  • Épée: A heavier weapon targeting the entire body, with points scored by the tip.
  • Sabre: A light cutting and thrusting weapon targeting the upper body, with points scored by the edge and tip.

Video courtesy of Olympics.com


History of Fencing

Fencing dates back to the earliest civilizations where swordplay was a key skill for warriors. It has been included in every modern Olympic Games since the first in 1896.

Basic Techniques

En Garde

The fundamental position from which all attacks and defenses are made.

Lunge

A quick forward movement to attack the opponent.

Parry

A defensive action where the fencer blocks the opponent's attack.

Riposte

An offensive action following a successful parry.
